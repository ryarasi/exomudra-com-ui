// Our Thesis

Bringing the machine to the body.

Every technology that touches us intimately has followed the same arc. It moved closer to the body. Speakers shrank from rooms to earbuds. Projectors became displays strapped to our faces. Content found the body. Control hasn't.

We still reach for keyboards, mice, and rigid controllers — machines that force our hands to conform to their shape. But almost everything we do to act on the world, we do with our hands.

Exomudra inverts the equation. We build smart gloves that map any hand gesture to any digital signal — structuring the machine around the hand, not the other way around. The applications are as limitless as what hands can do.

We're starting where it matters most: rehabilitation and patient monitoring for medical professionals, and sign language translation for the 70 million people the world still can't hear.

Healthcare & Rehabilitation

Active force feedback enables programmable resistance for finger strengthening. Therapists get objective range-of-motion and force data over time.

Sign Language Accessibility

Real-time gesture-to-text translation to bridge communication for 70M+ sign language users worldwide.

Precision Gesture Tracking

IMU + flex sensor fusion with proprietary algorithms for sub-centimeter finger tracking accuracy and ultra-low latency.

Active Force Feedback

Motor-driven resistance on each finger creates the sensation of gripping real objects in virtual space.

Cross-Platform

Windows, macOS, Linux. Unity, Unreal Engine, SteamVR, and mobile devices out of the box.

Fully Mobile

Self-contained wireless glove. No external trackers, no cameras, no tethers. Just put it on.
